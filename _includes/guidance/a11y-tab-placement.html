Placing tab controls below the content they manage creates a disconnect between the control and its content, resulting in a confusing and inaccessible experience across keyboard, mobile, and magnified views.

This pattern creates multiple usability and accessibility issues:

  • Breaks expected keyboard flow: Users navigate left-to-right, top-to-bottom. When tabs are at the bottom, focus moves away from the active panel instead of into it, forcing users to tab backwards which is not predictable and violates WCAG 3.2.3 Consistent Navigation, WCAG 2.4.3 Focus Order
  • Poor mobile usability: Content updates occur outside the user’s current view, making changes easy to miss.
  • Screen magnification barriers: Magnified users are likely focused on the tabs; updated content appears above and may not be visible, and users may scroll the wrong direction.
  • Focus and spatial disorientation: If panel height changes, activating a tab can shift the tabs’ position, causing users to lose their place.
  • Violates established tab design conventions: Tabs are expected to precede and control the content that follows.
