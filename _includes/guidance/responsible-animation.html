Responsible Animation

Be mindful of the impact that animation can have on people with disabilities. Animation can be a distraction for some, make others ill, and dangerous for those subject to seizures. By sticking to the following guidelines, we can make our web experiences pleasant and safe for everybody!

  1. Videos, CSS, gifs, or carousels that loop must include a pause button. Third-party plugins, such as Slick, have their own implementation of pause.
  2. Animations that occur when interacting with a web page are exempt from having a pause button if they do not last for more than 5 seconds. However, developers should continue to honor user settings and include prefers-reduced-motion to reduce or stop animation where needed. See MDN for more details about this useful media query.
  3. Animations that flash more than 3 times per second are not allowed on our websites, as they may induce seizures. A pause button will be ineffective here. If you suspect that animation is dangerous, please bring this to the attention of the Accessibility Team.
